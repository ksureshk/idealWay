STRUCTURE OF UCMAS:

For the first six months of the program the child is familiarized and made to operate the abacus. The beads on the abacus represent numbers and the central and the vertical rods provide additional value to the beads. The movement of the beads towards the central beam on a particular rod forms a picture and simultaneously a mathematical value. This association of the value or amount with the image of the bead formation on the abacus is imprinted into the child's mind.

a. FLASH CARDS: are used to enhance the visuals and help them memorize the pictures. By the end of the program they have 450,000 images stored in their memory.

b. ORALS: are used to foster their capability of listening skills excellent. They can enlarge their listening power by using some fraction of seconds memorize more numbers.

c. SPEED WRITING: is the method we approach to amplify their speed in a seconds calculation in their writing skills.

With the above methods, they child can express their like and creative skills.

Once they perfect their ability to recall these images and convert them into numbers, they are ready to attempt sums mentally with the help of the abacus — and without it after some time. It's possible to learn addition, subtraction, multiplication, exponents, factors and percentage on the abacus.

There's one more skill which gets honed as they learn the UCMAS. Using both hands to rapidly work on the beads on the abacus helps children to improve their motor skills, says Wong.

Moreover, their skill of picking up the "visual" aspects and process them improves, while mental maths only perks up their concentration and hearing.
